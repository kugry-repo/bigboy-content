## Ability Levels

Use this as a planning target across large sets of exercises:

- `application-directe`: direct use of a definition, formula, property, technique, or algorithm.
- `application-guidee`: usual application where the relevant knowledge is not stated explicitly.
- `synthese`: less usual situation requiring synthesis of several ideas.

Do not force every small set to match these percentages exactly.

## Official Unit Skill Map

### Limites Et Continuite

Typical skills:

- Calculate finite and infinite limits.
- Recognize indeterminate forms.
- Use factorization, conjugates, comparison, and standard limits.
- Study continuity at a point.
- Use the intermediate value theorem.
- Use strict monotonicity and continuity to prove uniqueness of a solution.

### Derivabilite Et Etude Des Fonctions

Typical skills:

- Study differentiability.
- Calculate derivatives.
- Use derivative signs to study variations.
- Build and interpret a variation table.
- Study extrema.
- Study convexity when required.
- Use derivatives to prove inequalities.
- Use function study to solve equations and inequalities.

### Suites Numeriques

Typical skills:

- Work with arithmetic and geometric sequences.
- Study recurrence sequences.
- Use monotonicity and boundedness.
- Determine limits.
- Use continuous functions in recurrence limits.
- Model simple problems using sequences.

### Fonctions Primitives

Typical skills:

- Recognize a primitive of a function.
- Determine primitives of usual functions.
- Use linearity of primitives.
- Prepare integral calculations.

### La Fonction Logarithme

Typical skills:

- Use properties of `ln`.
- Solve logarithmic equations and inequalities.
- Study limits involving `ln`.
- Connect logarithms to derivatives and function studies.

### La Fonction Exponentielle

Typical skills:

- Use properties of the exponential function.
- Solve exponential equations and inequalities.
- Study limits involving `exp`.
- Connect exponential functions to derivatives and differential equations.

### Calcul Integral

Typical skills:

- Calculate integrals using primitives.
- Use integration by parts.
- Use integral properties.
- Calculate areas between curves.
- Calculate volumes of revolution when required.

### Equations Differentielles

Typical skills:

- Solve equations of type `y' = ay + b`.
- Solve equations of type `y'' + ay' + by = 0` when required by the reference.
- Use initial conditions.

### Nombres Complexes - Partie 1

Typical skills:

- Use algebraic form.
- Calculate real and imaginary parts.
- Use conjugate, modulus, and simple algebraic operations.
- Solve quadratic equations in `\mathbb{C}` when appropriate.

### Nombres Complexes - Partie 2

Typical skills:

- Use modulus and argument.
- Convert between algebraic, trigonometric, and exponential forms.
- Translate geometric properties into complex-number language.
- Express transformations such as translation, homothety, and rotation.

### Geometrie Dans L'espace

Typical skills:

- Use vectors and coordinates in space.
- Express orthogonality using scalar product.
- Find plane equations.
- Find parametric representations of lines.
- Work with spheres.
- Use distance formulas.
- Use vector product when appropriate.
- Solve geometric position problems.

### Denombrement Et Probabilites

Typical skills:

- Choose an appropriate counting model.
- Calculate probabilities using union, complement, and intersection.
- Use conditional probability.
- Recognize independence.
- Determine a probability distribution.
- Calculate parameters of a random variable.
- Recognize and apply the binomial distribution.
