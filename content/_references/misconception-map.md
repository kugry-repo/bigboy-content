# Misconception Map

## Purpose

This file records common student misunderstandings and how lessons should address them.

The goal is to make lessons feel like they understand the learner.

Misconceptions should be treated directly in mini-lessons using:

- contrast blocks;
- wrong path then correction;
- `Le piège classique`;
- checkpoint questions;
- exam warning boxes.

## Limits and continuity examples

| Confusion | Why it happens | How to correct it | Suggested lesson block |
|---|---|---|---|
| Limite = valeur de la fonction | Students plug in directly and think the point value is everything. | Use a contrast block: limit near the point vs value at the point. | `Concept contrast` |
| $\frac{0}{0}$ means no limit | Students treat every calculation as a final answer. | Explain that $\frac{0}{0}$ is a signal to transform the expression. | `Le piège classique` |
| A function must be defined at $a$ to have a limit at $a$ | Students confuse behavior near a point with value at the point. | Use removable discontinuity examples. | `Le modèle mental` |
| TVI proves uniqueness | Students remember the theorem name but not what it gives. | Contrast existence vs uniqueness. | `Concept contrast` |
| Continuity is only drawing without lifting the pen | The visual image is useful but incomplete. | Keep the visual image, then add formal condition with limits. | `La version mathématique` |

## Derivatives examples

| Confusion | Why it happens | How to correct it | Suggested lesson block |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dérivée = equation of tangent | Students memorize tangent formulas without understanding rate of change. | Separate derivative value from tangent equation. | `Concept contrast` |
| Positive derivative means positive function | Students confuse sign of $f$ and sign of $f'$. | Use graph comparison and variation table. | `Le piège classique` |

## Probability examples

| Confusion | Why it happens | How to correct it | Suggested lesson block |
|---|---|---|---|
| $P(A \cap B)$ means $P(A)+P(B)$ | Students confuse intersection and union. | Use Venn diagrams and tree diagrams. | `Concept contrast` |
| Conditional probability is symmetric | Students think $P(B \mid A)=P(A \mid B)$. | Use a small real context with numbers. | `Le piège classique` |

## Complex numbers examples

| Confusion | Why it happens | How to correct it | Suggested lesson block |
|---|---|---|---|
| Module and argument are interchangeable | Students treat both as labels for a complex number. | Use geometric interpretation: distance vs angle. | `Concept contrast` |
| $|z_1+z_2|=|z_1|+|z_2|$ always | Students overgeneralize real-number rules. | Use counterexample and triangle inequality. | `Le piège classique` |
